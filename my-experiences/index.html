Project Coordinator

Hearts for Health, Since March 2022

I am collaborating with the Innovation team at Hearts for Health to initiate a new program, the Student Refugee Mentorship Initiative (SRMI). We seek to provide English tutoring to refugees and non-native speakers to enhance their conversational and reading skills. Our first cycle of mentor applications received nearly 150 applicants. We hope to reach 200+ mentees and support them in improving their ability to assimilate into work and education in the United States

The journey of starting a program completely from scratch has been thrilling, and a large learning process. Over the past few months, I have come to understand how to effectively outreach with organizations over phone and email, how to efficiently review large volumes of applications, as well as how to craft the right rubrics and interview questions to identify the best volunteers. I have also been able to develop my own self-confidence through several meetings with partner organizations, as I realize people are willing to truly listen to what I have to offer.

Board Member, Former CEO & CDO

EduVisa Learning, Since December 2020

When my high school transitioned to remote learning after the rise of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, I found myself gradually longing for the sense of community I would feel when attending school in person. After searching for online communities to replicate this sense of belonging, I stumbled upon EduVisa, a student-run organization which recreates this community through Discord, while also providing free academic tutoring, mentorship, and workshops to enhance our academic performance.

I initially was simply an audience member of EduVisa’s college admissions workshops and game nights. However, as I engaged more with other community members, I developed thrilling friendships with other high school students who related with me through these trying times. I sought opportunities to get involved to give back to this community, so I joined as a team member of the Engagement, Curriculum, and Outreach departments.

In the Engagement team, I was able to notice the direct impact that our events and programming had on building our community and furthering our academic journeys. As I learned skills in leadership, event planning, and organization, I rose as the Chief Development Officer and eventually the CEO. Through rapid transformations in departmental structure and the addition of a new College Admissions Series, I was able to increase our new member retention rate by 40% and expand our reach to over 3500 students.

Research Intern in Comparative Genomics

New York University Primatology Lab, June 2020 – August 2020

My internship at NYU’s Primatology Lab offered me an opportunity to translate the research skills I earned at Baruch into research in the biological sciences. Specifically, I analyzed the relative susceptibility for 15 catarrhine and platyrrhine species to develop a SARS-CoV-2 infection, alongside a team of a professor, 2 PhD mentors, and a fellow peer. We utilized bioinformatics software (NCBI BLAST, Unipro UGENE, and MAFFT) to perform multiple sequence alignment, so that we could easily compare the genetic variation in the genomes of each species at 12 key binding residues of the ACE2 gene. I concluded the project by presenting our research findings to university professors, graduate students, and the general public at our concluding colloquium.

Student Researcher in Personality Psychology

Baruch College STEP Academy, October 2019 – March 2020

My internship at Baruch STEP Academy was my very first research experience and helped to reaffirm my overall passion for analyzing data to find answers to my questions about human nature. I developed an independent research project which assessed how an individual’s personality traits can predict their optimal productivity methods and academic performance.

To accomplish this, I used a questionnaire of the Big Five Personality Factors to score 121 NYC high school students on key personality traits: openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, and neuroticism. I also used a sample questionnaire to score participants on Carson Tate’s four productivity profiles: Prioritizer, Planner, Arranger, and Visualizer. Finally, I used GPA as a measure of academic performance. All of these scores, along with information collected from demographical questions, were analyzed using statistical tests on SPSS.
